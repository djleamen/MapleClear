Canada Child Benefit (CCB)

Important: This page contains complex government language that MapleClear can help simplify. Click the MapleClear extension to get started.

Overview

The Canada Child Benefit (CCB) is a tax-free monthly payment made to eligible families to help with the cost of raising children under 18 years of age. The CCB might include the child disability benefit and any related provincial and territorial programs.

Eligibility criteria and entitlement determination

To be entitled to receive the CCB, you must meet all of the following conditions on the first day of the month for which the CRA calculates your entitlement:

  • you must be resident in Canada for income tax purposes
  • you or your spouse or common-law partner must be a Canadian citizen, a protected person, a temporary resident who has lived in Canada for the previous 18 months, or an Indian as defined under the Indian Act
  • you must be the primary caregiver of a child under the age of 18
  • the child must be resident in Canada, unless the child is only temporarily outside of Canada for a period of less than 12 months

Payment calculation methodology

The CCB is calculated based on your family's adjusted net income from the previous year, the number of children in your care, and their ages. The benefit is subject to income testing, which means that higher-income families receive a reduced benefit amount or may not be eligible at all.

For the 2023-24 benefit year (July 2023 to June 2024), you could receive a maximum of:

  • $7,437 per year ($619.75 per month) for each eligible child under the age of 6
  • $6,275 per year ($522.91 per month) for each eligible child aged 6 through 17

If your adjusted family net income is over $34,863, your CCB payments will be reduced. The reduction is calculated at a rate of 7% of the amount by which your adjusted family net income exceeds $34,863 for families with one child, plus an additional 13.5% for families with two children, plus an additional 19% for families with three children, and plus an additional 23% for families with four or more children.

Application process and required documentation

To apply for the CCB, you need to complete Form RC66, Canada Child Benefits Application. You must also provide supporting documents, including:

  • documents that prove the child's birth (such as a birth certificate or registration of birth)
  • documents that prove you live with the child
  • if you have a spouse or common-law partner, documents that prove their identity and your relationship status
  • if you or your spouse or common-law partner is not a Canadian citizen, documents that prove your status in Canada

Related programs and benefits coordination

The CCB may affect your eligibility for other benefits and credits. These include but are not limited to the GST/HST credit, provincial and territorial child benefits, and the Canada Workers Benefit (CWB). Additionally, if your child has a severe and prolonged mental or physical impairment, they may be eligible for the child disability benefit, which provides additional support.

Provincial and territorial program integration

Several provinces and territories have integrated their child benefit programs with the federal CCB. This means that when you apply for the CCB, you are automatically considered for provincial or territorial benefits. However, some provinces and territories require separate applications. You should contact your provincial or territorial government to understand what programs are available in your area and how to apply.

Payment schedule and direct deposit information

The CRA usually issues CCB payments on the 20th of each month. If the 20th falls on a weekend or statutory holiday, you will receive your payment on the next business day. To receive your payments, you must have direct deposit set up with the CRA. You can set up direct deposit through your CRA My Account online, by calling the CRA, or by submitting a direct deposit request form.

Income reporting obligations and recalculation procedures

Your CCB payments are based on information from your income tax and benefit return. It is important that you file your return by the due date each year, even if you have no income to report. If you do not file your return, your CCB payments will stop. Additionally, if your family situation changes (such as a change in marital status, number of children, or custody arrangements), you must inform the CRA immediately, as this may affect your benefit amount.

The CRA will recalculate your CCB entitlement based on your actual income when you file your tax return. If you received more than your correct entitlement, you may have to repay the overpayment. The CRA may recover overpayments by reducing future benefit payments or by other collection methods authorized under the Income Tax Act.

For more information or to apply, visit the CRA website at canada.ca or call 1-800-387-1193.
